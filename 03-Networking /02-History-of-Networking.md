# 🤔 How Did Networking Begin?

In the early days, computers were **large, expensive, and worked independently**.

They could not communicate or share information with other computers.

If someone wanted to transfer data, they often used storage devices like tapes or floppy disks.

This process was slow and inconvenient.

---

# 🌍 The Birth of Computer Networks

As technology improved, people wanted computers to communicate with each other.

Researchers started connecting computers using cables.

This allowed computers to exchange information quickly without physically moving storage devices.

This marked the beginning of computer networking.

---

# 🌐 ARPANET – The First Computer Network

In **1969**, the United States developed **ARPANET (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network)**.

ARPANET is considered the **first successful computer network** and the foundation of the modern Internet.

It connected computers at different universities and research centers.

---

# 🌎 The Internet

Over time, more computers and networks were connected.

Eventually, these networks formed one massive global network called the **Internet**.

Today, billions of devices communicate through the Internet every second.

---

# 📅 Networking Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1969 | ARPANET created |
| 1970s | More computer networks developed |
| 1983 | TCP/IP became the standard communication protocol |
| 1990 | World Wide Web (WWW) introduced |
| Today | Billions of devices connected through the Internet |

---

# 💡 Why Is Networking History Important?

Understanding the history of networking helps you understand:

- Why networks were created.
- How the Internet evolved.
- Why protocols like TCP/IP are important.
- How modern cybersecurity developed.

# 🌍 Real-Life Example

Imagine a village with only one road.

As the village grows, more roads are built to connect different places.

Eventually, all roads form a complete transportation network.

Computer networking evolved in the same way.
